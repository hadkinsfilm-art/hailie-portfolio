Artist Statement

Being a filmmaker, I strive to make art that is relatable to people of all backgrounds. My work primarily focuses on documentary and telling stories that could be labeled as "every day." Oftentimes in this world, we find ourselves consumed by several types of media. Because of this, my goal is to create work that moves a person emotionally and sticks with them throughout their lives. I have found that people are moved by what they can see themselves in. That is why I believe it is important to tell the not so glamorous stories but the ones that generate real, raw, emotional reactions in my audience.

For the creation of my work, I enjoy meeting people from different walks of life and framing a documentary or story about what I have gathered from their experiences, of course with their permission. My editing often leans to the more basic side of things, as I find it especially important to keep real life stories as realistic as possible on screen. I err on the simpler side of costume, makeup, and lighting for the same reason. However, I do feel it is vital that the story not only portrays reality but also is absolutely specific to the person whose story is being told. Therefore, I am flexible in cinematography to allow for each story to be unique.

I draw much of my inspiration from fictional but realistic stories. For example, the movie Eternity, written by Patrick Cunnane and written and directed by David Freyne, is a story about the afterlife. Despite the grandiose setting, the characters are only concerned with spending time with those they love. It is that relatable story in the midst of an unrealistic setting that brings the audience in and allows them to relate to people and places they have never seen before. It gives people a chance to see themselves in a stranger and to consider and observe that stranger's life but also to go home and consider and observe their own lives. That is entirely what I strive to do as a filmmaker.
